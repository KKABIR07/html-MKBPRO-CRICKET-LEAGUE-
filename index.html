Rule:

  1. Four-Run Limit:
    • A shot that crosses the boundary will be awarded a maximum of four runs.
    • Regardless of the distance the ball travels, only four runs will be credited to the batting team.
  2. Rule 2: No Sixes:
    • Scoring six runs for a shot is not allowed in this version of cricket.
    • Batsmen cannot score more than four runs on a single delivery.
    • 6=out
  3. Rule 3: Boundary Efficiency:
    • Batsmen are encouraged to focus on placement and timing to accumulate runs within the four-run limit.
    • Fielding teams can strategize to defend boundaries more effectively, knowing that sixes are not a scoring option.
  4. Rule 4: Bowler Empowerment:
    • Bowlers gain an advantage as the maximum runs per delivery are restricted, promoting accuracy and variety.
    • The absence of sixes allows bowlers to play a more central role in shaping the course of the game.

These modified rules aim to redefine the dynamics of cricket, emphasizing strategy, precision, and a balanced competition between bat and ball.
